🚀 Strategic Deterrence Report

India's Shield: Missile Power

From tactical supersonic cruise missiles like BrahMos to the intercontinental strategic deterrence of the Agni-V ICBM.

5,000+ Km ICBM Range
Mach 2.8 BrahMos Speed
400 Km AD Range

The Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme (IGMDP) initiated by late Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam set the foundation for India's missile prowess. Today, DRDL and BDlL lead the production of some of the world's most advanced missile systems.

Agni Series: The Strategic Deterrent

The Agni series consists of ballistic missiles with varying ranges, from Agni-I (700km) to Agni-V (5,500km). These are the pillars of India's nuclear deterrence, capable of reaching all major targets in the region. Agni-P (Prime) is the latest canister-ized variant featuring advanced navigation and MIRV potential.

BrahMos: The Supersonic King

A collaboration between India and Russia, the BrahMos is a ramjet-powered supersonic cruise missile that can be launched from land, air, sea, and sub-sea. It is widely considered the fastest and most lethal anti-ship cruise missile in operation today.

Akash: The Air Defense Umbrella

The **Akash Air Defense System** is a medium-range surface-to-air missile (SAM) capable of protecting large areas from air attacks. It features a sophisticated Rajendra radar and is increasingly being exported to partner nations under "Make in India."

Multi-Layered Shield

India is building a multi-layered air defense network to protect critical infrastructure:

  • S-400 Triumf (Long Range)
  • MRSAM / Barak-8 (Medium Range)
  • Akash-NG (Short-Medium)
  • QRSAM (Immediate Defense)
