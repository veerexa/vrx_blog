Gaganyaan: Tracking India's First Human Spaceflight Mission

Updated: March 2026 6 min read

The Gaganyaan mission represents ISRO's most ambitious project to date: sending a crew of three into a 400 km orbit for a 3-day mission and bringing them safely back to Earth.

Recent Progress

ISRO has successfully completed several "Test Vehicle" missions to validate the Crew Escape System (CES), which is critical for ensuring astronaut safety in case of any launch malfunctions.

  • Astronaut Selection: Four Indian Air Force pilots have been selected and completed rigorous training in Russia and India.
  • Life Support: Development of the Environmental Control and Life Support System (ECLSS) is in advanced stages to maintain a survivable environment in the capsule.
The Gaganyaan mission is not just a technology demonstration; it's about building long-term autonomy in space exploration.

FAQ

When is the first manned flight expected? +
Current estimates place the first manned flight in late 2025 or early 2026, following successful uncrewed trial flights.
